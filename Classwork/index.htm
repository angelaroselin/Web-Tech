About Me

I chose Babson College because I believe that Entrepreneurs are the future. My commitment to Babson College was heavily influenced by the Scholarship package offered, as I ultimately committed as a CWEL. As I was in the decision process, I took into consideration the liberal community and President Healyâ€™s commitment to a diverse environment, which I knew was critical for cultivating a Global mindset. I have always been attracted to things that are different than what I was used to, and this was an amazing opportunity to gain exposure to the actual business environment. I chose Babson College to further my education at the #1 School of Entrepreneurship in the United States, a decision I am more than happy with now, though I was unsure at the beginning. Babson was enticing for a few reasons. As a transfer student in high school, I understand the impact of small classes with a small teacher to student ratio. I also understand the importance of having a hands-on approach to teaching, and the value in seeing failures as learning opportunities. I think that in life, the thing we should fear is not failures, but our success at things that in the end, does not matter. I believe that having an entrepreneurial outlook on life will only be an asset later on. That level of thinking takes a certain kind of environment, and I believe that it is something applicable to all areas of life, not just in business and academics. When encountering a problem, go around it. Because I decided to take last semester off to launch a social entrepreneurship boot camp for high school students, I think that is a possible career route that I take on after college. I have always believed in the importance of education as one of the most powerful tools in the world, and I can see my involvement with my startup years after I graduate. However, because it is seasonal, I think I would be interested in taking on a consulting position as well. I hope that where ever I end up, I will be able to incorporate a social focus. My dream career, however, resides in eco-conscious tourism. On efforts to bridge cultures while increasing opportunities for locals.
